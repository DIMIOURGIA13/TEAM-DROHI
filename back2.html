Layman's Terms

- Web 3.0 is explained best through its features, namely ubiquity, decentralization, artificial intelligence, and semantic web interactivity.
Some Web 3.0 technologies have already emerged, such as the decentralized concept that underpins blockchain.
Other Web 3.0 meanings are yet to be understood, let alone created.

History of WEB 3.0

- Founded in 2014
- Founded by Gavin Wood, a co-founder of Ethereum
- Signified a decentralised, semantically aware version of the web
- Hence also known as Semantic Web
- Key development in 2017, starting to get associated with Blockchains; due to the increasing popularity of cryptocurrencies
